About Me

I am a health outcomes researcher and epidemiologist specializing in respiratory disease epidemiology, exposure assessment, and health policy evaluation. Currently, I am completing my MSc in Pharmaceutical Sciences at the University of British Columbia, where my research focuses on understanding the impact of air pollution on chronic respiratory diseases.

My research focuses on how air pollution—particularly PM2.5, NO₂, and O₃—affects asthma incidence and lung function. Using exposure-response modeling, systematic reviews, and cost-effectiveness analysis, I aim to inform evidence-based policies that mitigate air pollution’s health impacts.

Beyond research, I am committed to translating epidemiological data into real-world health strategies. My work has engaged policymakers, industry partners, and healthcare professionals, emphasizing its impact on public health planning. Looking ahead, I hope to bridge environmental health research with policy-driven interventions that improve respiratory health outcomes.

Research Projects

  • Systematic Review & Meta-Analysis on Air Pollution & Asthma Conducted a global meta-analysis on the impact of PM2.5, NO₂, SO₂, and O₃ on adult asthma incidence.
  • Cost-Effectiveness of Air Cleaner Rebates Developed a Markov model evaluating the economic feasibility of HEPA air cleaner rebates for asthma prevention.
  • Wildland Firefighter Health Risk Study Led a systematic review on the nutritional and health requirements of wildland firefighters exposed to smoke.
